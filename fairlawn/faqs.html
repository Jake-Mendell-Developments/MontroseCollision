Frequently Asked Questions

Body damage can come in all shapes and sizes. It could be serious if your vehicle was in a collision or a tree limb came down on it in a heavy storm, but it could also be very minor if there’s just a scratch or dent.

In either case, it’s important to know where to find a reliable collision center near Fairlawn, Akron, and Cleveland, OH. That’s where our team at Montrose Ford of Fairlawn can help. Our certified technicians can repair all kinds of collision damage.

What should I do in case I’m in an accident?

Accident scenes can be very shocking, especially right after the impact occurs. Try to remain calm and do the following:

  • Stop your vehicle. If you drive away, you could be charged with a “hit and run”.
  • If you are blocking traffic in a dangerous place, pull off to the side of the road or into a well-lit parking lot.
  • Contact the authorities. Try to assess the scene to see if anyone else is hurt. Wait for the ambulance and/or police.
  • Turn off your vehicle and wait for assistance.
  • Be courteous to all parties.
  • If you suspect the other driver might be drunk, do not confront him or her. Tell the police and let them handle the situation.
  • Make sure you are as calm as possible before making any statements or speaking to the police.
  • Take photos before you leave the scene.
  • Avoid making statements about responsibility to the other driver, witnesses, police or anyone else. Do not state that you are at fault, even if you think you may have caused the accident.
  • Contact your insurance company once you are calm and have gathered pertinent information. They will likely interview you on the spot and request photos.
  • Acquire a copy of the police report.
  • Call us for assistance with repairs to your vehicle.

What should I do after the accident?

  • Do not drive your vehicle if it is unsafe. If you can safely drive your vehicle, bring it to us as soon as possible.
  • If you cannot operate your vehicle, call us and we can tow your vehicle for you.
  • Remember that you have the right to go to the repair shop of your choice. Your insurance company or the other driver’s insurance company cannot require you to go to a particular repair shop.
  • Differences in repair shop estimates are common. It is your vehicle, you deserve the highest quality repairs. Read the fine print and consider carefully before you accept the lowest repair estimate.

What if my insurance company says I have to get 3 estimates or that I have to go to another repair shop and meet with their adjuster?

  • Tell them NO and you are dropping your car off at the shop of your choice to get repaired and the adjuster can inspect your vehicle with the body shop manager. It is your vehicle and you have the right to get your vehicle fixed where you want. We work for you, not the insurance company.

If my vehicle’s frame is damaged, is it a total loss?

  • No. A vehicle is a total loss only when the price of repairs exceeds the insurance company’s determined value of your vehicle. Nowadays, most vehicles are built using unibody (frame and body as one) construction. Our highly skilled technicians, with the aid of our state-of-the-art computer measuring system, can return your vehicle to its original factory specifications. If it is determined that the frame section is bent beyond repair, that section can simply be replaced.

We take special pride in our ability to match factory finishes as the final step in the restoration process.
